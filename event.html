Projects Undertaken


Snehaveedu (Project-1)

Snehaveedu (project - 1) was initiated by NSS SCTCE to provide homes for the needy. In collaboration with KTU-Chittilapally Foundation, we were able to undertake such a community project that would enhance our NSS volunteers in their personality development and creating a feel of empathy towards the needy people around us. We provided a house for a beneficiary residing in Neeramankara, TVM and the construction started on 17th January 2024.

Prakrithika (Organic Farming)

With the concept of "Haritha Vidyalam", an organic farming initiative, launched under the NRPF project named "Prakrithika" by the NSS units of SCT College of Engineering, aims to spread awareness among college students about the harmful effects of pesticides and fertilizers and to promote the importance of organic farming.

Blood Donation Camps

Blood donation camps are organized with the primary objective of collecting voluntary blood donations from individuals who willingly contribute to the community's blood supply. The pivotal purpose of these camps is to establish and maintain a readily available and sufficient blood reserve that can be accessed during medical emergencies, surgeries, and various healthcare requirements.

Kismath 3.0

Kismath began as an initiative by the NSS unit of SCTCE to engage the student community in social service while fostering a sense of unity and collaboration. The concept of a food fest was proposed as a means to leverage the diverse culinary talents within the student body to raise funds for charitable causes. The success of the first two iterations, Kismath 1.0 and 2.0, in generating significant funds for various NSS activities, motivated the planning of Kismath 3.0.

Biryani Fest

The aim of the NSS SCTCE Biriyani Fest was to raise funds for the house building project undertaken by NSS SCTCE. By organizing this event, the program aimed to mobilize resources to support the construction of houses for those in need within the community. Koode (Vishu Kit Distribution)

The primary aim of the event was to celebrate the Vishu festival by distributing Vishu kits to a few families in Pappanamcode, Malayinkeezh, and Neermankara. This initiative aimed to spread joy and hope to families in need, allowing them to partake in the traditional festivities despite their financial constraints. The event sought to embody the spirit of Vishu, which is a time of renewal and hope, and to extend this warmth to those who may otherwise be unable to celebrate due to economic hardships.

Velicham (LED Bulb Distribution)

NSS SCTCE undertook an initiative to distribute LED bulbs to underprivileged families in the Neeramankara and Malayinkeezh areas. The primary aim of this event was to provide access to energy-efficient lighting to improve the quality of life for those in need while promoting sustainable energy usage. By equipping these households with LED bulbs, the initiative sought to reduce their energy costs and contribute to a greener environment.

7-Day Annual Camp

Project Punarjani is a hospital renewing project undertaken by the volunteer students of NSS SCTCE, Trivandrum on their 7-day camp at Neyyar Dam Govt. Higher Secondary School. On this project around 60 volunteers up took the responsibility of renovating a primary health centre located at neyyar dam, Trivandrum. The hospital grounds were cleared and cleansed. Buildings and grounds were sanitized. There was terrible damage to the walls. The walls encircling the hospital grounds were cleaned and painted by us. In addition to the maintenance of the hospital, we also cleaned the school we were camping at. We cleansed the school premises and compost pits.
